## Post 1
**Date:** June 2026
**Topic:** Why "best [category]" listicles may be helping your competitors more than you in AI Overviews

During the last few years, the easiest GEO trick in the book was publishing a "best [category]" list and placing yourself at #1. It has worked to influence AI responses, and in many cases it still does. But it could also be handing the win to your competitors now, at least in AI Overviews.

I pulled 100 B2B "best [category]" queries from Google AI Overviews across three dates between April and June, and separated two of the main success metrics in AI search: being cited as a source vs. being actually recommended in the response. It turns out those results are very different.

When a brand's own self-promotional listicle was cited, that brand was left out of the actual recommendation 69% of the time. And the recommendations went to the established competitors they had listed in their own article. So you write a listicle, name your competitors, call yourself the best — and Google cites you as a source while recommending everyone else on your list. You've essentially accidentally advocated for your competition.

Google seems to have decoupled what it mentions from who it recommends, and recommendations appear to be anchored to real authority (who the rest of the web talks about and links to) rather than how many times you've called yourself #1.

If AI citations are your main KPI, I would reconsider. Being recommended is far more valuable.

## Post 2
**Date:** April 2026 (2 months ago)
**Topic:** The AI Slop Loop — how AI-generated misinformation becomes official narrative across LLM platforms

Last year, I asked Perplexity about the latest SEO/AI search updates. It confidently told me about the "Perspectives" core update from September 2025. The problem? That update never happened. It doesn't exist. It was fabricated by AI-generated content on agency blogs, cited by other AI-generated content, and eventually treated as fact by multiple LLMs.

To this day, you can ask ChatGPT, AI Overviews, or AI Mode and they will explain how this fictional update "fundamentally changed how search results rank."

This is what I call the AI Slop Loop: a hallucination gets scraped, republished, cited, and re-fed into the same systems that created it. The cycle compounds and the misinformation becomes the official narrative across AI platforms.

I spoke with journalists from the BBC and the New York Times about this problem and ran some experiments to test it. I published a fake core update announcement on my blog and within less than 24 hours, AI Overviews, AI Mode, Gemini, and ChatGPT were presenting it as fact. The results are alarming and the implications for the majority of AI users are not good.

## Post 3
**Date:** February 2026 (4 months ago)
**Topic:** Google's review system and why publishing listicles at scale is a high-risk content strategy

I think some marketers forgot — or perhaps never knew — that Google has specific guidance on how to write good reviews. This documentation originated from Google's Product Reviews Updates, a specific ranking system launched in 2023, later renamed the "Reviews System."

The reviews system is still an independent ranking system that updates on a regular and continuous basis. I worked on many sites affected by these updates. The results were brutal. If you didn't have substantial evidence of having tested the products and other first-hand experience and expertise signals, Google often wiped out the site's entire visibility.

So when sites write listicles — especially ones that rank their own company as the best — they are actually subjecting themselves to Google's criteria for high-quality reviews. And once you read them, you'll understand why it's nearly impossible to meet their criteria unless you've actually been a customer of every company or product you include in your review. How many companies have actually been customers of all the competitors they are reviewing in their listicles?

By introducing hundreds or thousands of review articles into a company blog, you are exposing the site to Google's brutal action against what it considers low-quality reviews. Write review content at your own risk.
