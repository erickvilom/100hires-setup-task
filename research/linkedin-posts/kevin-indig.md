## Post 1
**Date:** June 2026 (2 weeks ago)
**Topic:** AI search prompt tracking framework for B2B SaaS

Good prompt tracking starts with sample design. For your average B2B SaaS CRM category, I'd start with ~40 seed prompts: 12 brand, 12 category, and 16 problem prompts. The problem prompts matter because that is where purchase intent usually lives. Then I'd run each prompt 5x per platform every week. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews should each get their own score because aggregation hides how differently the engines behave. The panel also needs personas. CFO, IT, and marketing buyers evaluate the same CRM category through different criteria, so averaging them together hides where a brand actually wins or loses. The metric set should include mention rate, citation rate, average position, sentiment, and the attributes attached to each mention, all with confidence intervals. That is how prompt tracking becomes more accurate: each answer becomes one sample in a designed panel, not one truth in a dashboard.

## Post 2
**Date:** June 2026 (4 weeks ago)
**Topic:** SEO vs AEO — what's the same and what's different

Saying AEO and SEO are the same misses the point. Search rank still drives a lot of AI visibility, and most of the tactics overlap. But AEO is a different game. Same between SEO and AEO: 1. Technical accessibility: machines need to crawl, render, parse, and understand your content. 2. Clear information architecture: pages, entities, topics, and relationships need to be easy to map. 3. Content quality: thin, vague, generic content performs poorly in both systems. 4. Authority signals: credible sources, expert content, links, mentions, and citations still matter. 5. User intent matching: content needs to answer what people are actually trying to solve. New in AEO: 1. Rank matters after inclusion: in shortlists, first still wins. In narrative answers, mention quality matters. 2. Mentions can rival links: reviews, comparisons, forums, articles, and docs can shape the answer. 3. Prompts are harder to track: they're roughly 5x longer than keywords and harder to target page by page. 4. Positioning becomes machine-interpreted: AI infers your category, competitors, use cases, and buyer fit from the broader web. 5. Reputation gets compressed: owned content, third-party coverage, reviews, and consensus collapse into one answer. That's where HubSpot AEO comes in: it shows how your brand appears across AI search engines like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity, and gives marketers a way to improve visibility without treating AEO as a replacement for SEO.

## Post 3
**Date:** June 2026 (4 weeks ago)
**Topic:** AI Mode vs AI Overviews — different user behavior, different optimization strategy

The same Google user behaves differently in AI Overviews and AI Mode. I broke down a new clickstream study of 846k US Google sessions from Feb and March 2026. Prior public Google SERP mouse-tracking studies measured dozens of people. This one analyzed tens of thousands. The pattern: AI Mode is autoplay. AI Overviews is the Netflix browse. In AI Mode, users accept what they're handed. 88% take the AI's shortlist as-is, 74% pick the top result, and 64% click nothing at all. Closed loop. In AI Overviews, the same user starts comparing. Cursor coverage hits 83% of the visible page (vs. 66% without an AIO). Stillness rises from 29% to 44%. Back-scrolling makes up nearly half of all scroll activity. The user reads, weighs, scrolls past, then circles back. AI Mode optimization is a visibility problem at the model layer. AI Overview optimization is a comparison problem on the SERP itself.
